# SEO, Tracking & Ads — Complete A-to-Z Setup Guide

Everything below is a **manual, one-time dashboard step** (can't be done
from code) unless marked otherwise. Do them in this order — later steps
depend on earlier ones.

**Do this first, before anything in this guide:** deploy this update and
complete `SERVICE-ACCOUNT-SETUP-GUIDE.md` if you haven't already —
`sitemap.xml` and `product-feed.csv` are both generated by serverless
functions that need the service account to read your product data.

---

## Part 0 — Quick check: is everything live?

Open these three URLs (replace with your real domain) — all three should
load real content, not a 404:
- `https://your-site.vercel.app/sitemap.xml`
- `https://your-site.vercel.app/robots.txt`
- `https://your-site.vercel.app/product-feed.csv`

If any of them 404, the deploy didn't go through cleanly — check Vercel's
deployment logs before continuing.

## Part 1 — Google Search Console (free, do this first)

**What it does:** tells Google your site exists, shows you what search
queries bring people to your site, flags indexing/mobile issues, and lets
you submit your sitemap so new products get discovered faster.

1. Go to https://search.google.com/search-console
2. Click **Add Property** → choose **URL prefix** (not Domain — URL prefix
   is simpler for a Vercel-hosted site without custom DNS access) → enter
   `https://your-site.vercel.app` (or your custom domain if you have one)
3. Google will offer several verification methods. Pick **HTML tag**.
4. Copy the `<meta name="google-site-verification" content="...">` tag it
   gives you.
5. Open `index.html` in your project, find the comment block that says
   **"SEARCH ENGINE VERIFICATION TAG(S) GO HERE"** near the top, and paste
   the tag right there (replace the example line, or add below it).
6. Commit + push + let Vercel redeploy.
7. Back in Search Console, click **Verify**.
8. Once verified: left sidebar → **Sitemaps** → enter `sitemap.xml` → **Submit**.
9. Left sidebar → **URL Inspection** → paste your homepage URL → **Request Indexing** (does the same for a couple of your best product pages too, to speed up their first appearance in search).

**What to check periodically:** Search Console → **Performance** tab shows
which real Google searches are bringing people to your site, and their
position in results — this is your main free feedback loop for SEO.

## Part 2 — Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — customer tracking

**What it does:** shows you traffic sources (Google/Instagram/direct/etc),
which pages get viewed most, cart abandonment, and — once e-commerce
events are firing (they already are, this update wired them in) — which
products actually sell, not just get viewed.

1. Go to https://analytics.google.com
2. **Admin** (bottom-left gear icon) → **Create Property** → name it
   "AzubaTrends" → fill in your timezone (India) and currency (INR).
3. When asked for a platform, choose **Web**.
4. Enter your site URL and a stream name → **Create stream**.
5. You'll be shown a **Measurement ID** — looks like `G-XXXXXXXXXX`. Copy it.
6. Go to your Admin Panel → **Settings → Marketing** → paste it into
   **"Google Analytics 4 Measurement ID"** → Save.
7. That's it — no code editing needed, `js/tracking.js` picks this up automatically site-wide.

**Verify it's working:** In GA4, go to **Reports → Realtime**, then open
your live site in another tab and click around. You should see yourself
show up as an active user within ~30 seconds.

**What you'll be able to see once there's real traffic:**
- **Reports → Acquisition → Traffic acquisition** — where visitors came from (Google organic, Instagram, direct, referral, etc.)
- **Reports → Engagement → Pages and screens** — most-viewed pages/products
- **Reports → Monetization → E-commerce purchases** — this is your **"which product sells the most"** answer, broken down by product, automatically, because `purchase` events now carry item-level data
- **Explore → Funnel exploration** (build one manually) — see where people drop off between view → add to cart → checkout → purchase

## Part 3 — Meta Pixel (Facebook/Instagram tracking)

**What it does:** same concept as GA4 but for Meta's ecosystem — needed
for accurate Meta Ads targeting/retargeting and to measure Instagram/
Facebook-driven sales.

1. Go to https://business.facebook.com (Meta Business Suite)
2. If you don't have a Business Account yet, create one (needs a Facebook
   account, business name, your email).
3. Go to **Events Manager** (search it in the top search bar, or
   business.facebook.com/events_manager2).
4. Click **Connect Data Sources → Web → Meta Pixel** → Connect.
5. Name it (e.g. "AzubaTrends Pixel") → Continue → skip the "install code"
   step it offers (we're not using their snippet directly, our own
   `js/tracking.js` handles it) → Continue through to finish.
6. On the Pixel's overview/settings page, find the **Pixel ID** — a long
   numeric string.
7. Admin Panel → **Settings → Marketing** → paste into **"Meta Pixel ID"** → Save.

**Verify it's working:** Install the free **Meta Pixel Helper** Chrome
extension, visit your live site, and it should show a green checkmark with
`PageView` firing. Add something to cart and it should also show `AddToCart`.

## Part 4 — Google Merchant Center (Google Shopping listings)

**What it does:** gets your products showing up on the Google Shopping tab
and in Shopping-style results on regular Google Search — for free (organic
listings), and this is also the account paid Shopping Ads run through later.

1. Go to https://merchants.google.com → create an account.
2. Business information: fill in your business name, country (India),
   and — importantly — since this store is West-Bengal-delivery-only,
   you'll set shipping to reflect that in Step 5 below.
3. **Website claiming:** it'll ask you to verify + claim your website. If
   you already verified in Search Console with the same Google account,
   this step can often auto-verify — otherwise it'll walk you through a
   similar HTML-tag or DNS method.
4. Left menu → **Products → Feeds** → **+ Add feed**.
   - Choose **India** as target country, **English** as language.
   - Feed name: "AzubaTrends Products"
   - Input method: choose **Scheduled fetch**.
   - Feed URL: `https://your-site.vercel.app/product-feed.csv`
   - Set fetch frequency to **Daily** (keeps listings in sync with your
     real stock/price automatically — you never need to re-upload).
5. **Shipping settings** (Settings → Shipping and returns): set up a
   shipping rate/region that only covers **West Bengal** (matches your
   actual delivery area) — Google requires shipping info to be accurate;
   don't list nationwide if you don't deliver nationwide.
6. **Return policy** (Settings → Business information → Return policy):
   fill this in honestly based on what `terms.html` on your site says.
7. Submit for review. Google typically takes 3-7 days to approve a new
   Merchant Center account/feed. Common rejection reasons: mismatched
   price between feed and site (shouldn't happen here since it's the same
   Firestore data), missing return policy, or landing page issues — check
   the **Diagnostics** tab if something's rejected, it tells you exactly
   which products/fields have a problem.

Once approved, your products appear in Google Shopping search results **for
free** — no ad spend required for this baseline visibility.

## Part 5 — Meta Commerce Manager (Instagram/Facebook Shop + catalog)

**What it does:** powers an Instagram/Facebook Shop tab, and is required
for Meta's dynamic retargeting ads (showing someone the exact product they
viewed but didn't buy).

1. Go to https://business.facebook.com/commerce → **Get Started** → **Create a catalog**.
2. Catalog type: **E-commerce**.
3. Connect it to the same Business Account as your Pixel (Part 3).
4. **Add items → Use a data feed → Set a schedule**.
   - Feed URL: same one — `https://your-site.vercel.app/product-feed.csv`
   - Set daily updates.
5. Once items are loaded, go to **Catalog Settings → Instagram Shopping**
   and **Facebook Shop** to connect them to your actual Instagram
   business account / Facebook Page, if you want the storefront tabs
   (separate from ads — this is the free "browse and tap to buy" tab).
6. Meta reviews Shop-enabled catalogs too (can take a few days).

## Part 6 — Google Ads (Shopping/Performance Max campaigns)

**Do this only after Parts 2 and 4 are done and working** — you need GA4
(for conversion tracking) and Merchant Center (Shopping campaigns pull
directly from it) set up first, otherwise you'll be flying blind on
whether the ads actually produce sales.

1. Go to https://ads.google.com → create an account.
2. **Tools & Settings → Linked accounts → Google Analytics (GA4)** — link
   your GA4 property. This lets Google Ads import your `purchase` event
   as a conversion automatically, which is how it optimizes and how
   you'll measure ROI.
3. **Tools & Settings → Linked accounts → Merchant Center** — link the
   Merchant Center account from Part 4.
4. **Campaigns → New Campaign** → Goal: **Sales** → Campaign type:
   **Performance Max** (recommended for a small store — it automatically
   places ads across Search, Shopping, YouTube, Display using your
   product feed) or **Shopping** (more control, Search-results-only).
5. Set a **daily budget** — start small to test, e.g. ₹200-500/day, and
   watch results for 1-2 weeks before increasing.
6. Under **Conversion goals**, make sure "Purchase" (imported from GA4)
   is selected as what the campaign optimizes for.
7. Launch. Google typically takes a day or two to start serving ads at
   full volume ("learning phase").

**How to know it's working:** Google Ads → **Campaigns** → check the
"Conversions" column after a few days — this should roughly match new
orders in your Admin Panel that came in that window. Also check GA4 →
**Reports → Acquisition** — you'll see a new "Paid Search" / "Paid Shopping"
traffic source appear.

## Part 7 — Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram)

**Do this only after Part 3 (Pixel) and Part 5 (Catalog) are working.**

1. Go to https://business.facebook.com/adsmanager → **Create**.
2. Campaign objective: **Sales**.
3. Under "Conversion location," choose **Website**, and make sure your
   Pixel (Part 3) is selected as the data source, with **Purchase** as
   the conversion event.
4. For retargeting people who viewed but didn't buy: choose **Catalog
   sales** as the campaign type instead — pick your catalog from Part 5,
   and Meta will auto-generate ads per-product using your feed images/
   prices, targeted at people whose Pixel activity showed interest.
5. Set your budget (daily or lifetime), audience (age/location — remember
   to focus on West Bengal given your delivery area, plus a broader net
   if you want brand awareness beyond just conversion-ready buyers).
6. Ad creative: for Catalog Sales this is auto-generated from your feed;
   for a regular Sales campaign you'll upload your own image/video + copy.
7. Launch.

**How to know it's working:** Ads Manager's own "Results" column shows
Meta's count of purchases attributed to the ad. Cross-check against real
orders in Admin Panel — Meta's attribution window can sometimes overcount
slightly (it counts a purchase if someone clicked the ad up to 7 days
before buying, even if they came back organically the second time), so
treat it as directionally useful rather than exact.

## Ongoing maintenance — what needs zero further action

- `sitemap.xml`, `robots.txt`, `product-feed.csv` — all live-generated,
  update automatically as you add/edit/pause products in Admin Panel.
  Never re-upload anything manually.
- GA4 / Meta Pixel — once the two IDs are saved in Settings → Marketing,
  every page and every e-commerce event tracks automatically, site-wide.

## What still needs your attention periodically

- **Search Console → Performance** — check monthly to see which search
  terms are working, and consider writing more content/descriptions
  around your best-performing terms.
- **Merchant Center → Diagnostics** — check for any product-level
  rejections after adding new products.
- **Ad campaign budgets/performance** — review weekly for the first
  month of any new campaign, then monthly once it stabilizes. Pause or
  adjust anything spending without producing GA4-tracked purchases.
